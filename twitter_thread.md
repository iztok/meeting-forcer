**Tweet 2 — The real problem**

It's not that you forgot.

You saw the notification. You clicked "Later."

The problem is that joining a meeting requires a deliberate choice to *stop* what you're doing.

And when you're in flow... that's the hardest thing in the world.

---

**Tweet 3 — Failed solutions**

Calendar reminders? Clicked away.

Slack status? Ignored.

Asking a colleague to ping you? They forget too.

We keep trying to fix an attention problem with more notifications.

More notifications is exactly the wrong medicine.

---

**Tweet 4 — The insight**

What if your computer just... refused to let you keep working?

Not a banner. Not a sound. Not a popup in the corner.

A complete, total blackout of every screen.

The only way out: join the meeting.

---

**Tweet 5 — Introducing Meeting Forcer**

I built Meeting Forcer — a tiny macOS menu bar app that does one thing:

When your meeting starts → every screen goes black.

One button appears: "Join Meeting"

That's it. No escape. No procrastination. No "I'll join in a sec."

---

**Tweet 6 — How it works**

• Sits silently in your menu bar as 📅
• Reads your Google Calendar automatically
• Detects Google Meet, Zoom, Teams, and Webex links
• 1 minute before start: full blackout on ALL monitors
• Click "Join" → meeting opens, screen clears
• (There's a "Snooze 5 min" if you really need it)

---

**Tweet 7 — Setup in 3 commands**

```
git clone github.com/iztok/meeting-forcer
cd meeting-forcer
bash install.sh && ./run.sh
```

Works without Google Calendar too — add any meeting URL + time manually from the menu.
